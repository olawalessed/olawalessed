CopySeeker    

Copyseeker help users to generate converting contents for blogs, sales pitch, newsletters etc. 

Visit Development Page

Introduction

Copyseeker will be a platform for business owners, individuals, creatives and organizations to get access to auto-generated high conversion marketing copy and other content related materials. 

Idea

To design a platform where copy contents such as Headings, Ads, Blog content, Sales Pitch, Email Newsletters will be checked by professional copywriters to improve the conversion rate of the content and should be an automated process that makes the experience smooth for the users.

My Role

As part of the product team, I worked on the following aspect;

Brand Identity
Product Mapping
Interface Design
User Experience


I collaborated with a team that consist of;
1 Frontend Developer (React)
2 Backend Developer (Python)
5 Copywriters

The Constraint

The idea was tested offline with 5 Copywriters to help proofread and suggest fixes to copy contents from freelancers, small business owners and other users that work with contents.

While it sounds like a great idea, the product seems to create a huge problem for the admin, as it requires more hands if the user request increases. This eventually will create lateness in delivery time, high burnout rate for the proof-readers and generally give a bad total experience

Proffered Solution

To use AI/ML technology to substitute for the major part of the product, this will be used to complement human input on the product.
Creating a simple but yet powerful interface and user experience to improve user adoption of the platform

We developed a Structure

The team had a brainstorming session with a senior copywriter on the project, where we highlighted the major types of copy contents, such as Ads, Sales Pitch, Newsletters and the others, while focusing on one from the list which is Ads, we mapped out the types of ads based on the purpose as listed below
- Reach/Engagement
- Conversion
- Lead Generation
Also we studied the form (styles) which these ads can take such as listed below
- News Style
- Trigger Style
- Benefit Style
This helped us to draw out a map that is used to train the model that gives suggestions to users on how the copy they generate based on their input will perform due to focus on keywords.

Prototyping

Most of the hard work has been done on the backend, so, I decided to create a very simple interface that really hides the level of abstraction and which hels the user to use the platform during the testing period
Using Figma, I designed pages for the user to generate content based on the information provided, focusing on Ads for the MVP.
