Vendor-as-a-Service eCommerce

Nairatree will help users to invest in e-commerce without lifting any finger

Visit Development Page

About Nairatree

Nairatree is a vendor-as-a-service e-commerce platform that offers an easy way for individuals to become a vendor and get items from popular brands and sell to customers. The platform helps vendors to procure items from different stores around the world based on their requests, these items are made available on the Nairatree e-commerce platform for buyers. A vendor is only required to make a selection of items to procure, and the rest is been taken care of by the platform on automation.

Problem Statement

This project is aimed at designing a full custom eCommerce experience that will make it easier for the Admin, Vendors and the Buyers to use the Nairatree platform.

Scope & Constraints

The main scope of the product was to create the user facing part of the product, so that existing products can be sold to the customers of Nairatree.
The product feature was provided by the product owner and this helped in focusing on what to work on for the users and how to make each of these features usable.

The major setback was the plan of building the platform under 6 weeks, but building just the customer features does not reflect the MVP. The project eventually lasted Six (6) months.

The Process

From the documentation, key information about the product were indicated, this helped me to have more knowledge of what we are trying to build.
First, I created a mapping of how a user would use the platform to achieve basic tasks such as viewing information of a product.

Looking for Sources

Searching the internet on existing ecommerce platform, I noted likely pages that users would come across for a normal ecommerce platform. Using workflows from the sources I got, I was able to know how the vendor experience would fit into the experience.
I created a summary of the kinds of users that will be using the platform, I collected a simple assumption as to what could be the common functionality and experience that people would are used to and key pionts on how to improve these experience.

Ideation

I Checked out the top brands in the ecommerce industry both international and local
I checked behance for design inspirations and I create a moodboard
Worked on some ideas and shared with the engineering team

I was able to list the common attributes of the existing popular ecommerce brands, both international and local, this enabled me to have a foundational grasps of the visual elements that users are accustomed to. Also, from the moodboard I created from behance and dribble, I was able to find some new approach on how I can visually represent the information architecture to better improve the experience of users on the NairaTree platform. 

Design

I used Invision to do some of the basic layouts, and used the Device live preview to check the final look and feel of the interfaces as it would be displayed on an actual device.
After working on the Web and Mobile Version for the Admin, Vendor, and the User Screens. I transferred the whole design over to Figma. 

I use the following tools to achieve the final result
Adobe Illustrator for designing the brand identity for the ecommerce platform
InVision Studio for interface design.
Figma for final user experience

Outcome

The NairaTree platform will help individuals to invest their money in getting items and reselling conveniently, as the platform lift the heavy effort it takes to buy items internationally and selling local.
